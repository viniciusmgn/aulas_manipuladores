    CT Vector Fields


  • The (general) idea for computing the vector field at a configuration \(q\) is pretty simple, it is built as a vector sum:

                        \(f(q) = \underbrace{C(q)}_{ \substack{\text{Convergence} \\ \text{component}} } + \underbrace{T(q)}_{ \substack{\text{Traversal} \\ \text{component}} }\)


  • It works mainly because both terms are built so \(C(q)\) and \(T(q)\) are always orthogonal for all \(q\)!

                                                  \(g(u) \leq 0\)


  • \(u\) is the decision variable and models what we can use on the system to influence it

           \(f(u)\) is the objective function, and models what we want to do

           \(g(u)\) is the constraint function, and models the constraints that the system imposes to us
